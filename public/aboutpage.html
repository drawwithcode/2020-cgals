Memorypedia is a collaborative web experience about collective and personal memory, where
with a simple and instant interaction, users can contribute to a continuously growing visual narration
of words and images.

(1) SPECULATION — The name of the project comes from the semiotic concept of “encyclopaedia” identified
by Umberto Eco as the common array of all the knowledge and experiences shared by a group.
This common ground allows the comprehension of a message and if the speaker and the recipient do not
share the same encyclopedia, the message might be misinterpreted.
 This concept reflects on how communication is based on personal background and has an evocative
power that enables meanings that are not literal but come from the recipient or viewer’s personal experience.
The idea underlying the app is to play on this differences of interpretation.

(2) AIM — The huge amount of information provided bt the Internet encourages forgetting rather than remembering.
Creating a web app that reflects on memory could be seen as a provocative work with the aim of raising
awareness of the problem of how technology affects our capability of remembering.

(3) CONTEXT — A universitary project designed as a collaborative experience for sponsoring events related
to memory and communication, such as the Italian Festival della Memoria.

(4) PROCESS — The digital platform allows participants to take part in the experience anywhere, anytime.
By continuing the chain where it was left off, participants are able to collaborate with the rest of the world.

(5) OUTPUT — Memorypedia revisits a simple parlour game focusing on the psychology concept of involuntary
memory, that is our capability of creating links between different worlds by referring to our own experience.
The result is a chain of words and pictures, generated by all the contributions coming together.
